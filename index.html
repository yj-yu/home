Curriculum Vitae

Hi, I'm Youngjae Yu

I’m passionate about advancing embodied agents to understand and reason about the world.

I’m an Assistant Professor of Artificial Intelligence at Yonsei University, focusing on natural language processing, computer vision, and multimodal learning for AI agents. Before joining Yonsei, I was a researcher at the Allen Institute for AI working with Yejin Choi. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Seoul National University, advised by Gunhee Kim.

My research focuses on curating web, simulation, and real-world datasets while advancing multimodal large language models to build AI systems that are not only capable of understanding but also socially responsible. Along the way, I’ve been honored with the Best Paper Award at NAACL 2022, Outstanding Paper Awards at EMNLP 2023 and ACL 2024, as well as the Okawa Foundation Research Grant.

SW/HW codesign project

with Gangjae Jang, Jaemin Kim, Naehyuck Chang

Project "CodeWar" was developed using the PowerPC & MicroBlaze Virtex-5 Embedded Development Kit and bluetooth embedded module (FB755AC) for multiplay game.
In the recommendation of the professor, I developed the final term project and submitted it to the creative engineering design competition. The user must provide the analog signal of the Morse code controller as an input to support bombardment by unlocking all the given secret code faster than the competitor.
The Codewar game supports up to 2 multi-players and is designed to improve the morse code understanding of soldiers.
